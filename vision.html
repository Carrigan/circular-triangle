Why The Triangle?

  • The Triangle is well-positioned to be an innovative leader in circular development with the anchor of three world-class universities; close proximity of agriculture and natural resources; thriving business community; strong social sector and civic organizations; and cross-sector leaders interested in sustainability and equitable economic development.
  • The Triangle is also a magnet for innovative thinkers and doers. Little could spark the imagination and ingenuity of these entrepreneurs more than the chance to build something with global reach that will help people live, work and play in a way that will allow us to prosper in the long term.

Lets Start in Durham

  • Durham is ready to take advantage of the transition to a more circular economy, as it forms a key part of North Carolina's vibrant Triangle region.
  • Durham is actively reimagining itself during the recent period of growth by revitalizing its downtown and cultivating new assets in the arts as well as in high-tech innovation. This growth creates great opportunity but also challenges in equitable housing and jobs, construction waste, consumer consumption and waste, and increased traffic and congestion.
  • The area supports a thriving life-sciences sector based in the Research Triangle Park and backed by leading universities.
  • The creativity and entrepreneurship of the city's residents are key attributes that facilitate the creation of a localized circular economy that propels Durham into a equitable and resilient future.
Our Vision
The Triangle is a global leader in innovation for the circular economy providing solutions for environmental, social, and economic challenges. We identify and implement circular solutions regionally that lead to a cleaner environment and inclusive economy. The result is a resilient and equitable community.
Zero Waste
  • Reduces our climate impact by conserving natural resources and reducing pollution from extraction, manufacturing and disposal.
  • Creates jobs with up to 10 times more jobs than disposal from collecting and handling recyclable materials to new business models to support sharing, reuse, repair, and rental. Local money is spent on local jobs.
100% Renewable
  • Reduces greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution by generating energy that produces no greenhouse gas emissions and eliminating fossil fuels
  • Creates high quality jobs and economic development in manufacturing, installation, and maintenance of new technology
Resilient
  • Increasing green space leveraging necessary buffer zones for extreme heat, drought, floods, and other natural events to benefit the community with ready access to nature and recreation
  • Promotes self reliance and security by diversifying energy supply and reducing dependence on imported fuels
Shared Prosperity
  • Skill development, training, and entrepreneurship for new jobs for circular industry and innovative businesses supported by skill development, training, and inclusive programs designed to lift up those who have traditionally been excluded.

Next Steps
1. Further Discussions with Experts
Invite leading thinkers in circular design, architecture, agriculture, and construction for 1:1 visits for deeper exploration. Includes IDEO, Gehl, Metabolic, Bill McDonough.
2. Community Engagement
A conference and learning series will create a shared vision, attract global leaders to highlight best practices, share study results to ground the discussion in reality, and run workshops to create a roadmap for a Circular Triangle.
3. Experiential Tour - The Netherlands
This in-country experience will allow leaders to explore the circular economy with specific operational examples at the municipal, building, and product levels. We will create a foundation for dialogue and discussion, as well as help translating the experience back to the unique local context of Durham.
4. Circularity Study
The study, commissioned by Durham and conducted by global experts, will identify the regions best opportunities to create and lead in a circular economy.
